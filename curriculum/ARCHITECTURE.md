## PROGRAM PHILOSOPHY

**Andragogical Foundation (Knowles)**
Every module is self-directed, problem-centered, experience-anchored, and intrinsically motivated. No lesson exists without a business reason. No technical output exists without a market output.

**Geopolitical Frame (Dan Wang)**
Students aren't learning to get jobs. They're choosing to engineer the future — to be the builders that tip the civilizational competition toward democratic, autonomous, agentic superpowers.

**YC Integration**
YC Startup School provides the founder operating system. Every YC module maps to a program phase. The agentic cohort replaces the YC group partner — available 24/7, infinitely patient, ruthlessly honest.

**Greedy Token Philosophy**
Students ship MULTIPLE products throughout the program. Every technical concept is learned in the context of building something real. The capstone isn't a project — it's a VC-ready company.

## AGENTIC PEER COHORT — AGENT ROSTER

## CURRICULUM ARCHITECTURE

### PRE-PROGRAM: Founder Onboarding (Week 0)
**YC Integration:** Modules 1–2 (Deciding, Ideating)
**Andragogical Entry Point:** Establish learner's need to know, prior experience, readiness

**Technical:**
- Environment setup: GitHub, cloud accounts, local dev
- First agent interaction: define your problem hypothesis

**Business:**
- YC: "Should You Start A Startup?" + "Before the Startup" (PG essay)
- Complete Andragogical Learner Analysis (Knowles Ch. 18)
- Define: Problem, ICP, Why Now, Why You

**Agentic Cohort:**
- Scout challenges your ICP assumptions
- Sage surfaces your existing experience as a resource
- Vera asks: "Would I fund this problem?"

**Gate:** Students who can't articulate a crisp problem + ICP don't advance. The agents help them find one — they don't move on with a weak hypothesis.

---

### PHASE 1: Foundation Stack (Weeks 1–4)
**Theme:** Build the engineering foundation your startup needs — nothing more, nothing less.

#### Module 1: AI-Assisted Development & Prompting
*Original curriculum: AI-Assisted Software Development + Prompt Engineering*
**Reframe:** "How do I use AI to build 10x faster than a traditional developer?"

- Technical outputs: Functional AI-assisted codebase, prompt library
- Business output: First prototype of your product concept
- YC tie-in: "How to Build an MVP" — your prototype IS your MVP hypothesis
- Agent: Reed (rubber duck), Arch (architecture review)

#### Module 2: Web Application Foundation
*Original curriculum: Web Application and Interface Design*
**Reframe:** "What does your user actually touch? Build it."

- Technical outputs: Full-stack web app — frontend, backend, database, API
- Business output: Clickable prototype for user testing
- YC tie-in: "How to Talk to Users" — deploy to 5 real users this week
- Agent: Scout (who are your users?), Arch (is this the right stack?)

#### Module 3: Data Engineering & Databases
*Original curriculum: Relational Databases + NEW: Data Engineering Pipeline*
**Reframe:** "Your AI is only as good as your data. Build the pipeline."

- Technical outputs: Relational DB design, data pipeline, preprocessing
- Business output: Data moat hypothesis — what data do you collect that competitors can't?
- YC tie-in: PG "Do Things That Don't Scale" — your data advantage starts manual
- Agent: Mo (is this production-grade?), Vera (what's your data moat?)

#### Module 4: Software Testing, Git & CI/CD
*Original curriculum: Software Testing and CI/CD*
**Reframe:** "Ship fast without breaking things. This is how startups stay alive."

- Technical outputs: Test suite, GitHub workflow, CI/CD pipeline
- Business output: Deployment cadence — ship to users weekly minimum
- YC tie-in: "How to Launch (Again and Again)"
- Agent: Mo (production readiness), Reed (test coverage review)

**Phase 1 Milestone:** Working product in users' hands. At least 5 people have used it. You have feedback. The agents have stress-tested your architecture and your market assumptions.

---

### PHASE 2: AI Engineering Core (Weeks 5–10)
**Theme:** Make your product genuinely AI-native. Not AI-sprinkled — AI-core.

#### Module 5: LLM-Based Applications & RAG
*Original curriculum: LLM-Based Apps*
**Reframe:** "How does your product use language models to deliver value no non-AI product can?"

- Technical outputs: LLM app with RAG pipeline, vector database integration
- Business output: AI value proposition — articulate the specific job your LLM does
- YC tie-in: "Startup Business Models and Pricing" — what does your AI feature enable you to charge for?
- Agent: Arch (RAG architecture), Vera (is this a feature or a company?)

#### Module 6: AI Agents — Design & Implementation
*Original curriculum: AI Agents — EXPANDED*
**Reframe:** "Agentic systems are the product. You're not adding an agent — you're building one."

- Technical outputs: Single-agent system, multi-agent orchestration, tool use, memory
- Business output: Agent product specification — what decisions does your agent make autonomously?
- YC tie-in: "Where Do Great Startup Ideas Come From?" — agents create new categories
- Agent: Arch (agent architecture), Sage (meta — you are building what you're learning with)

#### Module 7: Machine Learning & Model Fine-Tuning
*Original curriculum: ML to AI Model Fine-Tuning — 8 courses*
**Reframe:** "When off-the-shelf models fail your product, you fine-tune. Here's how."

- Technical outputs: Trained ML models, fine-tuned LLM on your product's domain
- Business output: Model differentiation thesis — why is your fine-tuned model a moat?
- YC tie-in: PG "How to Get Startup Ideas" — proprietary models = defensible products
- Agent: Mo (model ops, deployment), Vera (is fine-tuning your moat or a distraction?)

#### Module 8: Production Observability & Evals
*NEW — replaces Managing AI App Dev II*
**Reframe:** "Your AI product in production will behave unexpectedly. Measure everything."

- Technical outputs: Eval framework, monitoring dashboard, alerting system
- Business output: KPI dashboard — the metrics that tell you your product is working
- YC tie-in: "How to Set KPIs and Prioritize Your Time"
- Agent: Mo (production health), Scout (are you measuring the right things for your users?)

**Phase 2 Milestone:** AI-native product deployed. Users are getting value from the AI features specifically. You have evals running. You know your model's failure modes.

---

### PHASE 3: Scale Architecture (Weeks 11–14)
**Theme:** Build for the scale you're pitching VCs you'll reach.

#### Module 9: Cloud Services & Architecture
*Original curriculum: Cloud Foundations + Cloud Services & Architectures*
**Reframe:** "What does your infrastructure look like at 10x, 100x, 1000x users?"

- Technical outputs: Cloud-deployed architecture, cost model, scaling plan
- Business output: Unit economics — cost per user at scale
- YC tie-in: "Growth for Startups" — growth requires infrastructure that doesn't collapse
- Agent: Arch (architecture review), Vera (what's your cost structure at scale?)

#### Module 10: Microservices & Kubernetes
*Original curriculum: Microservices Architectures*
**Reframe:** "Decouple your system so you can ship faster and scale independently."

- Technical outputs: Containerized microservices, Kubernetes deployment
- Business output: Engineering team scaling plan — what does your first engineering hire own?
- YC tie-in: "All About Co-Founders" — how do you build a team around this architecture?
- Agent: Mo (deployment), Arch (service boundaries)

#### Module 11: Software Design & Architecture Patterns
*Original curriculum: Software Design and Architecture*
**Reframe:** "Design systems that new engineers can understand and extend."

- Technical outputs: UML diagrams, documented architecture, design patterns implemented
- Business output: Technical due diligence package — what a VC's technical advisor will review
- YC tie-in: "How Startup Fundraising Works" — VCs do technical DD
- Agent: Arch (the hardest review of the program), Reed (can you explain this to a new hire?)

**Phase 3 Milestone:** Architecture is VC-presentable. You can articulate your technical decisions and their tradeoffs. You have unit economics. You have a scaling plan.

---

### PHASE 4: Go-To-Market & Fundraise (Weeks 15–17)
**Theme:** Engineer the raise. This is the product launch.

#### Module 12: AI & Organizational Transformation
*Original curriculum: AI and Organizational Transformation*
**Reframe:** "How does your product change how your customers operate? That's your pitch."

- Technical outputs: Integration architecture (how your product plugs into customer systems)
- Business output: Customer transformation narrative — before/after your product exists
- YC tie-in: "Startup Business Models and Pricing" + "Growth for Startups"
- Agent: Scout (customer discovery), Vera (is this a painkiller or a vitamin?)

#### Module 13: Ethics, Governance & Professionalism
*Original curriculum: Professionalism and Ethics*
**Reframe:** "AI-native companies that ignore ethics get regulated out of existence or canceled."

- Technical outputs: Bias audit, data governance framework, responsible AI checklist
- Business output: Ethics and governance narrative for investor decks
- YC tie-in: Implicit in all YC content — build something people actually want AND trust
- Agent: Sage (the deepest conversation of the program)

#### Module 14: VC Fundraising & Company Building
*Original curriculum: Capstone — REPLACED*
**Reframe:** "You're not presenting a project. You're pitching a company."

- Technical outputs: Full deployed product, GitHub repo, architecture documentation
- Business output: Investor deck, financial model, fundraising strategy, YC application-ready materials
- YC tie-in: Modules 7–8 (Fundraising + Founder Stories) — full integration
- Agent: Vera runs a full partner-level pitch simulation. All agents present in final review.

**Final Milestone / "Graduation":**
- Deployed AI-native product with real users
- Revenue or clear path to revenue
- VC-ready pitch deck and materials
- Multiple additional products shipped throughout program (greedy token)
- You can answer: "What's your moat?" without hesitation
